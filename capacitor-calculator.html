Formulas

E = (C × V²) / 2 — energy stored in capacitor (joules) τ = R × C — time constant (seconds) I₀ = V / R — peak current at t = 0 (amps) P₀ = V² / R — peak power dissipated at t = 0 (watts) V_charge(t) = V × (1 − e^(−t/τ)) — voltage while charging V_discharge(t) = V × e^(−t/τ) — voltage while discharging I(t) = (V/R) × e^(−t/τ) — current magnitude (same for charging & discharging)

V = applied voltage (volts)  |  C = capacitance (farads)  |  R = resistance (ohms) τ = time constant (seconds)  |  E = stored energy (joules) At t = 1τ the capacitor is ~63.2% charged; at t = 5τ it is considered fully charged (>99%).
